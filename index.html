The Origin — A Boy With Eyes That See Tomorrow

Jaisen Hizaki — born in Tokyo, 5'10", told he was too short, too thin. But inside him lived a gift: Precognition. He saw the game three seconds into the future. By 13, he was scouted for Japan's National Junior Team. But no one could catch his passes.

The 2017 National Japan Cup Finals — championship on the line. Jaisen threaded a perfect dime. It rattled out. They lost by two. That night, he swore: "I will build a kingdom of players who can match my vision."

🔍 The Search — Five Corners of the World

The Japan All-Stars were born. Average margin of victory: 50 points.

July 2, 2021 — The Disbandment. Jaisen's final words: "Wherever you go... don't embarrass the name." Five directions. And in the shadows: Tekkai Black watched.

Before the All-Stars, there were two: Jaisen and Tekkai. Best friends. Then Jaisen chose Julian. Tekkai vanished to America, became a Top 5 prospect, and mastered "Copy". Now he's back.
